## Project Overview
This project integrates employee performance and benefit data to optimize HR strategy at **Tech Innovators Inc.**  
Using Looker Studio dashboards, correlations between attendance, training, and performance were analyzed while tracking benefit usage and satisfaction.  
Insights enabled proactive HR interventions and resource allocation.

---

## Business Introduction
**Tech Innovators Inc.**, founded in 2015, is a New York-based technology and consulting firm serving SMBs across North America.  
The company specializes in IT solutions, custom software, and cloud services, employing over 200 staff.  
Recent investments in employee development and remote work infrastructure highlighted the need for performance and benefits analytics.

---

## Business Problem
Challenges included:
- Fragmented performance data across departments, limiting visibility  
- Underutilized employee benefit programs with unknown ROI  
- Delays in identifying at-risk employees and optimizing development programs  

**Impact:**
- Operational inefficiencies in performance management  
- Rising HR costs from underused benefits  
- Employee dissatisfaction, lower engagement, and turnover risk  

---

## Rationale for the Project
The project aimed to:
- Provide real-time HR visibility  
- Enable data-driven decisions for performance and benefit optimization  
- Improve employee engagement, satisfaction, and retention  
- Support strategic workforce scaling  

---

## Aim of Project
Enable HR and leadership to make timely, data-driven decisions on employee development, performance management, and benefits optimization by integrating fragmented datasets into real-time dashboards.

---

## Project Objectives
- Centralize performance and benefit data across departments  
- Analyze correlations between attendance, training, and performance  
- Identify low-performing benefit programs and optimize allocation  
- Flag at-risk employees for proactive interventions  
- Provide department- and location-specific insights to HR and leadership  

---

## Data Sources
- **Employee Performance Data:** Employee ID, Department, Role, Quarterly Performance Rating, Attendance, Training Completion, Project Delivery Success, Engagement Score  
- **Employee Benefits Data:** Benefit Type, Usage Frequency, Satisfaction  
- **Employee Demographics:** Age, Gender, Tenure, Location  

---

## Tools Used
- **Looker Studio:** Real-time dashboards, analytics, visualization  
- **Google Sheets:** Raw HR data extraction and preprocessing  

---

## Data Cleaning & Preparation
- Consolidated fragmented performance and benefit datasets  
- Standardized department and location naming  
- Handled missing or inconsistent performance ratings and attendance data  
- Prepared benefit usage metrics for ROI and satisfaction analysis  

---

## Exploratory Data Analysis
- Analyzed performance trends by department and location  
- Examined correlations between attendance, training completion, and performance  
- Reviewed employee benefit usage and satisfaction rates  
- Identified high-risk employees for proactive management  

---

## Data Analysis
- Aggregated quarterly performance ratings and attendance  
- Linked benefit usage and satisfaction to performance outcomes  
- Visualized risk flags and top-performing employees by department  
- Tracked low-ROI benefits to inform resource reallocation

---

## Recommendations
- Reallocate underutilized benefits to high-impact programs  
- Implement targeted training for at-risk employees in Sales and Operations  
- Continue real-time performance monitoring using Looker dashboards  
- Align benefit offerings with department-specific needs and engagement trends  

---

## Limitations
- Dependent on timely and accurate HR data input  
- Performance ratings may be influenced by subjective manager assessments  
- Benefit usage satisfaction surveys may have response bias  
- Dashboards require continuous maintenance to remain actionable
